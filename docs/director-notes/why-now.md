# Why this play, now, in Lausanne, in 2026

Pirandello wrote it just after a world war, in a country that didn't quite know what it was, in a theatre culture that had become comfortable with sentiment and craft. He wrote a play that said: *your craft cannot hold what is happening. Your forms are not equal to the times. Your tools cannot tell the truth about people.*

A hundred years later, in a country that has become very good at staying comfortable and a continent that has become very good at not looking, that play has become more relevant, not less.

The Step-Daughter walks on stage and demands to be heard, and Europe, in 2026, is full of step-daughters demanding to be heard, and full of audiences and institutions deciding which ones we will dignify with the word *real*.

The play is not topical. It is permanent. But this is a good time to stage it.
