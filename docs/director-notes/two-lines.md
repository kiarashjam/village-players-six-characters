# Two lines

## The line to protect at all costs

The Mother's *It's taking place now. It happens all the time.*

Twelve words. It's the only sentence in the play that isn't trying to be theatre. Cut any speech you need to cut, push any register you want to push, modernise anything that wants modernising — but those twelve words have to land cold and exact. They are the play's centre of gravity.

## The line to watch most carefully for tonal drift

The Manager's *I've lost a whole day over these people.*

It's the funniest line in the play and also the cruellest. If the actor playing the Manager goes for the comedy too hard, the cruelty disappears and the play ends sentimental. If the actor goes for the cruelty, the comedy disappears and the play ends preachy.

The right reading is both at once, and the only way to find it is in the room with the actor — but you should know going in that this line is where the whole evening's tone gets settled.
