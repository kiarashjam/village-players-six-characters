# Further threads

Three conversations not had here. Pull on any of them if useful.

1. **The play's inheritance.** Why *Six Characters* has been so important to the playwrights who came after — Beckett, Genet, Pinter, Stoppard, Churchill, Crimp, Vogel all owe it something. What each took, what each didn't.

2. **Why it tends to be staged badly in English.** The metaphysical vocabulary of the philosophy does not translate well; English wants its philosophers to be funny and Pirandello's Father isn't funny in the right way. What the Storer translation does and doesn't do, and where the modernised English in this edition is trying to fix it.

3. **Pirandello's biography.** His wife's mental illness, his complicated relationship with fascism, what he did and didn't compromise — and how much of it bleeds into the writing.
